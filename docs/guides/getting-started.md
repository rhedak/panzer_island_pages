# Getting started

Panzer Island plays differently from most turn-based strategy games. This guide covers the core ideas you need to get through Chapter 1, without going into the mechanical depth of the [Reactive Turns](reactive_turns.md) guide.

---

## The one thing to understand first

**You have no fixed movement range.** Your units can reach any walkable cell on the map in a single action. The constraint is not distance. It is the damage you absorb along the way.

Every step you take triggers a reaction from nearby drones. A guard tower fires every time you step into its range. A patrol chases you. A sentinel that was dormant wakes up because another drone fired nearby. Distance costs health, not actions.

This changes how you think about the map. The question is never "can I reach that tile?" It is "how much fire do I take getting there, and is it worth it?"

---

## The route preview

Before you commit to a move, the game shows you a route preview. Tiles highlighted in orange are dangerous: at least one drone will fire on your unit if it crosses them. The preview also shows a damage estimate for the full route.

Use it. Always check the preview before moving into an unknown area. The preview also shows which drones would be alerted by your movement, marked by an orange outline around their detection zone.

---

## Your three units

You start Chapter 1 with Katyusha. Nadeshiko and Maria join in stages 2 and 3.

**Katyusha** is your tank. She takes hits well and hits back hard. Put her in front. When drones are firing at her and she has Iron Curtain ready, activate it: she will counter-attack up to three drones that fire on her while the shield holds.

**Nadeshiko** flies. She crosses water, mountains, and any terrain the other units cannot enter. She is fragile, but her Storm Run limit break can cut through a cluster of drones in one move. Use her to reach isolated drones, flank positions, or deliver a dash through a tightly packed formation.

**Maria** requires water tiles to move, but her range and firepower make her strong in any coastal stage. Park her on water in range of a priority target and let her work. Her Broadside limit break fires a 3x3 barrage and suppresses drone counterfire while it lands. It clears formations cleanly.

---

## Spreading the damage

Drones fire at the unit that moved or attacked, but at the end of every action, any drone that did not react will fire at your other units if they are in range. Leaving Nadeshiko parked next to a cruiser while Katyusha moves will cost Nadeshiko health.

Rotate which unit advances so no single unit absorbs all the fire. Keep units out of drone range when they are not the active unit, when possible.

---

## Limit breaks

Each unit has a limit gauge that fills as they deal and take damage. When full, you can use a limit break instead of a normal action.

- **Iron Curtain** (Katyusha): blocks incoming damage and counter-attacks drones that fire on her. Best used when you are advancing into a cluster of drones.
- **Storm Run** (Nadeshiko): dashes in a straight line and damages every drone adjacent to the path. Best used to clear a fortified position or reach a high-value target.
- **Broadside** (Maria): 3x3 barrage, no drone counterfire. Best used to clear a packed area or break a defensive formation.

Limit gauges reset at the start of each stage but can refill during a stage. In a multi-sector stage, consider saving a limit break for the later sector.

---

## Priority targets

Not all drones are equal. Two types amplify the threat of everything around them:

**Relay nodes** increase the attack range of nearby drones while they are alive. If a guard tower seems to be hitting you from an unusual distance, look for a relay node nearby. Destroy it first: its death immediately reverts the boosted drones.

**Spotters** mark units in range, increasing damage they take from other drones. Destroying a spotter reduces incoming damage significantly.

**Detonators** pursue the nearest unit and self-destruct on contact, dealing area damage. They have very low HP and die in one hit. Kill them before they close the gap.

---

## Multi-sector stages

Some stages are split into sectors. You advance to the next sector after clearing the current one. Your units carry their current HP and limit gauges into the next sector, but destroyed units do not return.

Position your units carefully before the sector transition. HP you carry forward is HP you did not waste.

---

## The core principles

1. Read the route preview before committing to a move.
2. Rotate which unit advances to spread the damage.
3. Destroy relay nodes and spotters before engaging the drones they support.
4. Save a limit break for the start of a difficult sector.
5. Keep fragile units (Nadeshiko) out of drone range when they are not acting.
